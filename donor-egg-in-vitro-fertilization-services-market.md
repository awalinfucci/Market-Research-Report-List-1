<p><p>The Donor Egg In Vitro Fertilization (IVF) services market is primarily divided into two types: Fresh Donor Egg IVF Cycle and Frozen Donor Egg IVF Cycle. In a Fresh Donor Egg IVF Cycle, oocytes are harvested from a donor and used immediately for fertilization, offering higher success rates but requiring synchronization of donor and recipient cycles. In contrast, the Frozen Donor Egg IVF Cycle involves the cryopreservation of eggs for later use, providing flexibility and convenience for recipients without the need for immediate donor involvement.</p></p>
