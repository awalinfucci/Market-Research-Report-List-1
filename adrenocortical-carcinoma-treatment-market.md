<p><p>The adrenocortical carcinoma treatment market is primarily categorized into two types: localized and metastatic adrenocortical carcinoma. Localized adrenocortical carcinoma refers to cancer that has not spread beyond the adrenal gland, often treated with surgical resection, radiation, and targeted therapies. In contrast, metastatic adrenocortical carcinoma has spread to distant organs, necessitating more aggressive treatments such as systemic therapies, chemotherapy, and immunotherapy. The distinction between these types influences treatment strategies and market dynamics, impacting drug development and clinical approaches.</p></p>
