<p><ul><li>Upright Fluorescence Microscopy</li><li>Inverted Fluorescence Microscopy</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The fluorescence microscopy market comprises primarily two types: upright and inverted fluorescence microscopy. Upright fluorescence microscopes are designed for observing samples mounted on slides from above, making them suitable for thick specimens and applications where objective access is crucial. In contrast, inverted fluorescence microscopes allow for viewing samples from below, ideal for examining living cells in culture dishes. Each type serves specific research needs, facilitating advancements in cell biology, materials science, and medical diagnostics through enhanced imaging capabilities.</p></p>
