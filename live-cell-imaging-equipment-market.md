<p><p>Live cell imaging equipment is crucial for studying cellular processes in real-time. Confocal laser scanning microscopes use laser light to focus on specific layers of a specimen, yielding high-resolution images and allowing for the observation of dynamic cellular activities. In contrast, spinning-disk confocal microscopes utilize multiple lasers and a rotating disk with pinholes, enabling faster imaging and reduced phototoxicity, making them ideal for capturing rapid cellular events and monitoring live interactions in greater detail.</p></p>
