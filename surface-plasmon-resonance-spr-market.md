<p><p>Surface Plasmon Resonance (SPR) is a powerful analytical technique used to study molecular interactions in real-time without the need for labeling. It operates on the principle of measuring changes in the refractive index near a metal surface, typically gold, when biomolecules bind to immobilized ligands. This allows researchers to assess binding kinetics, affinity, and concentration of analytes, making it invaluable in fields such as biochemistry, pharmaceuticals, and environmental science.</p><p>The SPR market is experiencing significant growth due to increasing demand for advanced diagnostic tools and rising investments in research and development.
