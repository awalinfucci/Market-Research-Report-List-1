<p><ul><li>Barium-based Contrast Media</li><li>Iodinated Contrast Media</li><li>Gadolinium-based Contrast Media</li><li>Microbubble Contrast Media</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The oral contrast agent market comprises various types used in imaging procedures. Barium-based contrast media enhance gastrointestinal imaging through better visualization of the digestive tract. Iodinated contrast media are primarily used in X-ray and CT scans for vascular and organ imaging, while gadolinium-based contrast agents are utilized in MRI for soft tissue visualization. Microbubble contrast media, often used in ultrasound, improve blood flow and organ imaging by enhancing the reflectance of ultrasound waves, thereby providing clearer diagnostic images.</p></p>
