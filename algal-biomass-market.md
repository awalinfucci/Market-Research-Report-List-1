<p><p>The algal biomass market is categorized into three main types: microalgae, macroalgae, and cyanobacteria. Microalgae, composed of unicellular organisms, are rich in proteins and omega-3 fatty acids, making them valuable for food supplements and biofuels. Macroalgae, or seaweeds, are larger and utilized in food products, pharmaceuticals, and biofertilizers. Cyanobacteria, often referred to as blue-green algae, are prominent for their high productivity and potential in biofuel production. Other market segments may include various algal species and hybrid systems for diverse applications.</p></p>
